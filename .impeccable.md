# Portfolio — design context

Sourced from `/impeccable teach` (owner input). Update this file when positioning changes.

## Design Context

### Users

- **Primary audience:** Hiring managers.
- **Context:** Mixed — quick scans and occasional deeper reads (device and situation vary).
- **Job to be done:** Answer “how good is this candidate?” fast — credibility, scope of work, and proof without hunting.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **direct**, **minimal**, **unpretentious**.

Voice is straightforward, not stiff or corporate. The site should feel approachable and human, not like a formal résumé brochure.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Visual tone:** Minimal — restraint over decoration; clear hierarchy, generous negative space where it helps scanability.
- **Theme:** **Dark only.** No requirement to design or maintain a light theme for this product.
- **Color:** Keep the **current OKLCH token system** and overall palette; evolve incrementally rather than replacing with a new scheme unless the brief changes.
- **References:** None specified (owner: “idk”).
- **Anti-references:** Overly serious, corporate, or “safe” generic portfolio aesthetics.

### Design Principles

1. **Hiring-manager scan first** — Above the fold and section headers should communicate role, level, and proof paths (work, OSS, activity) without fluff.
2. **Minimal surface area** — Fewer elements, each earning its place; avoid decorative patterns that do not add signal.
3. **Direct copy and structure** — Short labels, concrete outcomes, no hedging intros that repeat the heading.
4. **Dark-only execution** — Layout, contrast, and charts are judged on dark surfaces only.
5. **Preserve the existing palette** — Treat current tokens as the brand baseline until explicitly revised.

### Constraints (stated by owner)

- Formal accessibility conformance (e.g. WCAG), reduced-motion policy, and related inclusion work are **not current priorities** for this personal site. Revisit if audience or goals change.
